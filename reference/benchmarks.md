# Industry Benchmarks — Independent Venue Operators

Source: Compiled from Australian hospitality industry standards, BDO Australia Hospitality Benchmarking Reports, and Profitable Hospitality benchmarks. These are targets for healthy independent venues — not averages (averages include struggling businesses).

---

## Food Cost %

| Category | Healthy | Watch | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Specialty coffee / espresso drinks | 18–25% | 26–30% | 31%+ |
| Food (café / brunch) | 28–33% | 34–38% | 39%+ |
| Food (full-service restaurant) | 28–35% | 36–40% | 41%+ |
| Alcohol / beer / wine | 20–28% | 29–34% | 35%+ |
| Cocktails / mixed drinks | 15–22% | 23–28% | 29%+ |
| Packaged / retail products | 35–45% | 46–55% | 56%+ |

**Note:** Food cost % = COGS ÷ selling price × 100. Always use selling price inclusive of GST/VAT if your costs include it, or exclusive if they don't. Be consistent.

---

## Labour Cost %

| Venue Type | Healthy | Watch | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Café (counter service) | 30–35% | 36–40% | 41%+ |
| Café with kitchen (brunch / lunch) | 33–38% | 39–44% | 45%+ |
| Full-service restaurant | 35–40% | 41–46% | 47%+ |
| Bar / venue (evenings) | 30–38% | 39–44% | 45%+ |

**Labour % = total wages (including super/on-costs) ÷ total revenue × 100**

Include all staff: kitchen, front of house, management. Exclude owner drawings unless the owner is replacing a paid role.

---

## Combined COGS (Food + Labour)

Prime cost = food cost % + labour cost %. This is the most important single number in a hospitality P&L.

| Venue Type | Healthy | Watch | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Café | 58–65% | 66–72% | 73%+ |
| Full-service restaurant | 60–68% | 69–75% | 76%+ |

If prime cost exceeds 70–72%, there is no path to a healthy net profit at typical venue rent and overhead levels.

---

## Other Key Ratios

| Line Item | Healthy | Watch | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rent / occupancy | Under 8% of revenue | 8–12% | 13%+ |
| Gross profit (after food cost only) | 65–75% | 55–64% | Under 55% |
| EBITDA / net profit | 10–15% | 5–9% | Under 5% |
| Waste / spoilage | Under 2% of food purchases | 2–4% | 5%+ |

## Revenue per Labour Hour

A quick diagnostic for labour efficiency. Divide total weekly revenue by total hours worked (all staff).

| Result | What it means |
|---|---|
| $60+/hr | Strong productivity |
| $45–$60/hr | Acceptable |
| $35–$45/hr | Likely over-staffed for revenue level |
| Under $35/hr | Significant problem — roster or revenue issue |

## Menu Item Volume Thresholds (for menu engineering)

"High popularity" vs "low popularity" depends on the total number of items on your menu.

A rough guide: if a menu item accounts for less than 70% of its category's average covers, it's low popularity.

Example: You sell 10 menu items. Total weekly covers = 500. Average per item = 50. Low popularity threshold = 35 covers/week.
