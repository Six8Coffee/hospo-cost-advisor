# Menu Engineering — Classification Framework

Based on the Kasavana & Smith matrix (1982), adapted for independent venue operators.

---

## The four quadrants

Classification is based on two variables:
- **Popularity** — covers per week
- **Contribution margin** — selling price − COGS

```
                    HIGH MARGIN        LOW MARGIN
                 ┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
HIGH POPULARITY  │   ⭐ STAR        │   🐴 PLOWHORSE  │
                 ├─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
LOW POPULARITY   │   🧩 PUZZLE      │   🐕 DOG        │
                 └─────────────────┴─────────────────┘
```

---

## Classification thresholds

- **High/low margin threshold:** average contribution margin across all items
- **High/low popularity threshold:** 70% of average covers per item across all items

---

## Actions by classification

| Type | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ⭐ Star | High popularity, high margin | Protect. Feature prominently. Never discount. |
| 🐴 Plowhorse | High popularity, low margin | Raise price by $1–2, reduce COGS, or bundle with a high-margin item. Do not cut. |
| 🧩 Puzzle | Low popularity, high margin | Reposition on menu, improve description, use staff selling. Reclassify as Dog after 2 failed menu cycles. |
| 🐕 Dog | Low popularity, low margin | Cut. Exception: retain one Dog per dietary category (GF, vegan) only if removing it loses a customer segment — but renegotiate the recipe. |

---

## Priority order for action

1. Cut Dogs
2. Reprice Plowhorses
3. Promote Puzzles
4. Protect Stars
