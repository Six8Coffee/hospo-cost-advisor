# Hospo Cost Advisor

A folder-based AI specialist for independent café and restaurant operators who want to understand and improve their food costs, labour costs, and menu profitability — without a finance degree.

## How to use it

Create a new Claude project (claude.ai → Projects → New project), then upload each file in this folder individually as project context — all seven files, including the three inside reference/. Start a conversation. No setup required — the specialist already knows what benchmarks apply, how to cost a menu item, and how to classify your menu. Give it real numbers and it will give you a real answer.

## What to ask

- *"My flat white costs $1.05 to make and sells for $5.50. Is that healthy?"*
- *"My labour last month was $16,400 on $44,000 revenue. That feels too high — is it?"*
- *"Here are my top ten menu items and their costs. Tell me what to cut, reprice, and protect."*
- *"I want to understand why my café isn't profitable even though we're busy."*

## What to expect

The specialist will ask for your numbers if you don't provide them, benchmark your results against Australian independent venue targets, and give you a direct verdict with a specific action. It won't give generic advice. It won't moralise. It will tell you what the number means and what to do about it.

## What's in this folder

| File | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `identity.md` | Who the specialist is and what they cover |
| `rules.md` | How they respond — format, tone, constraints |
| `examples.md` | Three example interactions showing the specialist in action |
| `reference/benchmarks.md` | Australian independent venue benchmarks for food cost, labour, and profit |
| `reference/menu-engineering.md` | The Stars / Plowhorses / Puzzles / Dogs classification framework |
| `reference/cogs-template.md` | How to cost any menu item from scratch, with worked examples |

## Keeping it current

When you learn something new about a niche — a better benchmark, a framework that works — update the relevant file in `reference/`. The specialist updates automatically next time the folder is loaded.
